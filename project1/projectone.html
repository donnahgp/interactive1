"2004. What's an anime?"

My first anime experience started around 2004, when my friend talked about a show with cat teaching ballet. She introduced me to my TV's On Demand function where we would end up watching an anime called Princess Tutu together. It was the first time I heard about anime and I loved it. Since then, I would end up watching anime using the On Demand function on my TV when my favorite cartoons werenâ€™t on.
Some notable animes I watched during this time included Princess Tutu, Hell Girl, and Pokemon.

2004~2005. The DVD Discovery

After discovering anime, I wanted to see if my library had anime DVDs I could borrow and watch. This is where I discovered my favorite anime at the time, Fushigi Yuugi. I would end up borrowing the same Fushigi Yuugi DVDs all the time from the library.
The other anime I ended up getting DVDs was Cardcaptor Sakura! Although I don't remember finishing the first episode.

2005. As Seen on TV

Every saturday night at 9 PM, two episodes of Naruto would air for one hour, and it was the highlight of my week, every week, for as long as I could remember. I even had a Naruto headband that I bought at the mall, figurines, posters and even books from the Naruto series. Waiting an episode on TV was another way of how I watched anime before the internet.
Other notable animes I watched on TV included Magical DoReMi and Death Note. (Although Naruto will always be my #1).

2006~2007. The Internet

Finally, we've reached the age of the internet, where I was able to watch an endless amount of anime using a variety of shady websites that would give me viruses and questionable ads. The internet allowed me to discover so much more anime beyond what I saw on TV. The first anime I finished through the internet was Vampire Knight!
Other notable animes I watched in the early internet days included Shugo Chara, K-On, and Lucky Star.

This is a collection of the animes I've watched and has shaped my childhood, beginning from pre-internet watching methods to my early internet days. It includes On Demand TV functions, DVDs, Cable TV, and early internet.
